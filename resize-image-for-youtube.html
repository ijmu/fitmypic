Video thumbnail sizing

Resize Image for YouTube Thumbnail

Recommended thumbnail size

A common YouTube thumbnail size is 1280 x 720 pixels, which is a 16:9 aspect ratio. This size works well for video previews and keeps the image sharp across desktop and mobile screens.

Starting with a larger 16:9 source image is ideal. If the source is vertical or square, use a crop or background before exporting the final thumbnail.

Make text readable

Thumbnail text should be large enough to read on a phone. Avoid placing important words near the bottom right, where interface elements may cover part of the image.

Strong contrast between text and background helps the thumbnail stand out. Use fewer words and leave enough space around faces, products, and key objects.

Use the preset

Upload your image, click the YouTube Thumbnail preset, and export as JPG or WebP for a smaller file. If your source image is not 16:9, crop or reposition it before final publishing.

Resize to 1280 x 720

Practical checklist

  • Use 1280 x 720 pixels for a standard 16:9 thumbnail.
  • Keep the main subject visible at small sizes.
  • Avoid tiny text, thin fonts, and low-contrast colors.
  • Export a compressed JPG or WebP when the platform allows it.

FAQ

Can I use a square photo for a YouTube thumbnail?

You can, but it should be cropped, padded, or redesigned into a 16:9 canvas before final export.

Why does text look blurry?

The source may be too small, the text may be thin, or the export quality may be too low. Use a larger source and increase export quality.

Should I export PNG?

PNG is fine for graphics with sharp text, but JPG or WebP is often smaller for photo-based thumbnails.
